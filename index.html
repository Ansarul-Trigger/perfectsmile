What I Wish I Knew About Veneers

So you’re thinking about veneers? Maybe you’ve already had a consultation, or you’ve watched enough smile makeover content to know what you don’t want, but the more you research, the more confusing it gets.

That’s because the veneer industry is full of flawless before-and-afters... and very little honesty about what can go wrong.

As someone who’s had veneers (and did a lot of research beforehand), here are five red flags I wish more people talked about — and how to protect yourself from them.

1. Aggressive Tooth Filing

You’ve probably seen the “shark teeth” trend online, where natural teeth are filed down to tiny pegs before veneers or crowns are applied. Here’s the issue: that’s not how veneers should be done.

Tooth Prep Type What It Means Safe for Veneers?
Minimal prep Only a thin layer of enamel removed Yes – industry standard
Aggressive prep Tooth filed down to a stub; irreversible No – unless medically necessary
“Turkey teeth” prep Extreme filing for speed/convenience abroad High risk of nerve damage

If your dentist is suggesting extensive filing for purely cosmetic veneers, walk away. Learn more

2. One-Size-Fits-All Smiles

A good veneer case is personalised, not based on a default “ Hollywood smile.” Your face shape, skin tone, bite, and even your speech patterns all influence how your smile should be designed.

Design Approach What to Expect Result
Custom smile design Digital mock-ups, facial scans, shade selection Natural, face-fitting result
Copy-paste smile Same 8 veneers on every patient Flat, artificial, or generic

If the dentist doesn’t ask about what you want — or doesn’t offer mock-ups or try-ins — they’re not tailoring the result to your face.

3. Too Cheap to Be True

Porcelain veneers are a premium treatment, not a bargain buy. If a clinic is offering complete set at a suspiciously low price, ask yourself: what’s being compromised?

Veneer Price (Per Tooth) What It Usually Means
Under £300 Likely composite or overseas lab with no follow-up
£500–£900 Composite in UK — less durable, quicker application
£1,000–£1,200+ High-grade porcelain, custom lab work, UK-registered dentist

It’s not just the material — it’s the technique, lab quality, bite adjustment, and long-term care that count.

4. No Mention of Aftercare

Veneers need maintenance. They don’t get cavities, but the teeth beneath them can — and without follow-up hygiene, even the most beautiful set will fail over time.

Aftercare You Should Expect Why It Matters
Hygiene clean every 3–6 months Keeps gums healthy and prevents hidden plaque build-up
Night guard (if you grind) Prevents cracks, chips, or premature wear
Bite checks after fitting Ensures comfort and long-term functionality

If the clinic emphasises the speed or ease of the procedure but fails to mention long-term care, be cautious.

5. No Questions Asked

A reputable dentist will ask about your lifestyle, habits, goals, and fears before offering treatment. If they don’t ask about things like:

  • Your bite and how you chew
  • Whether you grind your teeth
  • What kind of result you hope for
  • Your expectations or budget

…then they’re not building a treatment plan around you — just selling a product.

What They Should Ask Why It’s Important
Do you grind or clench your teeth? May affect material choice or require a guard
Do you want a natural or enhanced look? Affects shape, shade, and finish
How do you feel about your current smile? Helps guide emotional goals and desired outcome

Final takeaway

Veneers can be life-changing — but only if done safely, ethically, and with your long-term wellbeing in mind.

Look for a clinic that:

  • Doesn’t rush your decision
  • Offers you a trial smile or design preview
  • Talks about bite, speech, and maintenance
  • Respects your face, not just your wallet

A real smile transformation isn’t fast or flashy. It’s thoughtful. It’s tailored. And it’s done with care. Our cosmetic dentists know that all too well.
